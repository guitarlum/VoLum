Same POST section as Frame 3 — only the chain focus moved. DELAY now has the amber underline, its controls fill the bottom row, REVERB stays active (LED lit) but its knobs are out of view until you click it back.

How to read this mockup

Triptych: the central row has three sections — PRE, AMP, POST — and exactly one is expanded at a time. The collapsed two become 70 px vertical strips. Click a strip to expand it; the previously expanded one collapses. AMP collapse keeps a mini fern + caption so you always see the loaded amp.

Chain row: only appears when PRE or POST is expanded. Each effect is a placeholder square (the real art will be a procedural fractal in the same family as the amp art — Logistic map / Lichtenberg / Cantor dust, drawn at runtime). Connected by thin teal hairlines in signal-flow order. The currently focused effect has a thin amber underline; its knobs fill the bottom row.

Bottom knob row: "replace mode" — never shows two effects' knobs at the same time. AMP shows the six existing knobs + CHANNEL stepper. PRE shows the focused booster's knobs. POST shows the focused POST effect's knobs.

Bypass: each effect has its own slide switch (same amber style as NOISE GATE / EQ today), shown in the knob row when that effect is focused. The chain LED in the corner of each placeholder square mirrors the bypass state at a glance.

NOISE GATE / EQ: always visible across all four frames, beneath the knob row, exactly as today. They belong to the amp signal path, not to PRE/POST.

Default state: Frame 1 — AMP expanded, no effects active in PRE or POST, plugin opens like today's UI plus two slim navigation tabs.
