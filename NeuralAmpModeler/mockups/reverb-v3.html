1
PRE collapsed-but-active strip (70 px). Mini-glyph stack inside shows the boost is engaged. Section LED at top is teal. Click anywhere on the strip to expand PRE; click the LED only to group-bypass all PRE effects without expanding.
2
AMP collapsed-active strip (70 px). Always 70 px because the amp is always running. Mini fern + caption preserve rig identity ("Brunetti XL 2") even when not focused.
3
POST expanded section head. Section LED + label + active-effects breadcrumb ("REVERB"). When delay also engages, this becomes "REVERB · DELAY" (Detail B).
4
Per-effect mini-LED inside the mini-glyph. Mirrors that effect's individual bypass state. Lets you read "boost on, reverb off" at a glance from collapsed sections — no need to expand to know what's making sound.
5
Chain row above the knobs. One placeholder square per active POST effect (here just REVERB). The focused effect gets an amber underline; its controls fill the bottom knob row. Hover state shows an amber border + chevron beneath (see Frame 4's REVERB glyph).
6
Bottom knob row, replace mode. While REVERB is focused, the row is MIX / DECAY / TONE — the existing CHANNEL/INPUT/GATE/BASS/MID/TREBLE/OUTPUT knobs are out of view (visible by clicking AMP). The trade-off you accepted earlier: tweaking amp tone + reverb at once requires section flips.
7
Per-effect bypass slide switch. Same amber-on-dark style as today's NOISE GATE / EQ toggles. Toggling here mutes just reverb and leaves the rest of POST alone. Independent from the section group-bypass at #1.
8
Algorithm picker. Vertical column of 4 mode labels (SPRING, PLATE, HALL, SHIMMER). Active mode highlighted in amber. One click swaps the underlying DSP topology — Mix / Decay / Tone semantics stay constant across modes.
9
Footer status hint. Always-visible summary of what's running across all sections, even when sections are collapsed. Helps recall "wait, did I leave reverb on?" without scanning every strip.

Five things changed from v2

1. Dormant tabs shrunk 70 → 30 px. Default-state hero is back to its current 540 px width. PRE/POST tabs now cost essentially nothing visually until something gets engaged in them.

2. Dormant tabs use teal corner-brackets. Same vocabulary as the selected-amp row in the sidebar, so they feel native to the existing UI rather than bolted on.

3. Chain glyph hover state. See Frame 4's REVERB glyph: amber border + chevron beneath signals "click me to swap focus." Static mockup can only show this on one item at a time, but the real implementation applies it on hover for every chain item.

4. Section LED is now a 3-state group-bypass affordance. Dim grey = section dormant; teal = section has at least one active effect; amber (not shown in any frame) = section has effects but they're all currently muted as a group. Click the section LED on a collapsed strip to toggle group-bypass without expanding. Stage-friendly, recording-friendly.

5. Active-but-collapsed mini-glyph stack. Frames 3 and 4 show PRE collapsed at 70 px with a BOOST mini-glyph inside the strip. Per-effect LED on the mini-glyph mirrors the individual bypass state. You can read the full pedalboard state from the collapsed strips alone.
